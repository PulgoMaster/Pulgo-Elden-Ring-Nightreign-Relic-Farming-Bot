SECTION C — DEEP RELIC CURSE STRUCTURE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Curses ONLY exist on Deep of Night relics.
Normal relics never have curses (all curse table IDs = -1 in the datamine).

─── C1. HOW CURSES ARE ASSIGNED ─────────────────────────────────────

Curses are NOT assigned by a separate random roll after purchase.
The number of curses is determined when the relic's base entry is selected
from the ItemTableParam chain. Each relic entry has fixed curse slots.

Each curse slot draws independently from the CURSE POOL (TABLE_3000000):
  24 curses, all equal weight (100 / 2400 = 4.167% each per slot).
  Duplicate curses are possible (same curse can fill two slots).

─── C2. THE PASSIVE-CURSE PAIRING RULE ──────────────────────────────

This is the key structural insight:
  EACH CURSE SLOT IS PERMANENTLY PAIRED 1-TO-1 WITH ONE EXCLUSIVE PASSIVE SLOT.

The exclusive pool (TABLE_2000000) contains the 49 high-tier passives unique to
Deep relics. The broad pool (TABLE_2100000) contains the 277 mid-tier passives.

  0 curses = all passive slots draw from the BROAD pool (TABLE_2100000)
  1 curse  = 1 slot from EXCLUSIVE (TABLE_2000000) + remaining from BROAD
  2 curses = 2 slots from EXCLUSIVE + remaining from BROAD
  3 curses = all 3 slots from EXCLUSIVE (only possible on Grand relics)

Consequence: ANY relic carrying an exclusive passive (PA+3, PA+4, Max HP,
Improved Affinity Attack Power +2, etc.) ALWAYS has at least one curse.
You cannot get an exclusive passive without a curse.

Conversely, a 0-curse relic can ONLY have broad-pool passives.

SECTION F — CURSE FILTER PROBABILITY IMPACT
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

When you block N curses in the Curse Filter, it reduces the effective
probability of finding a matching relic because relics that carry a
blocked curse are rejected even if they have the right passives.

Minimum 3 curses must remain unblocked (enforced by the bot).
This prevents the impossible case of a 3-curse Grand relic having
no valid curses to draw from.

Probability that a Deep relic PASSES the curse filter (no blocked curse appears):

  Blocked   Remain   Overall   Delicate  Polished  Grand
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
     0         24    100.00%   100.00%   100.00%  100.00%
     1         23     96.30%    97.92%    96.28%   95.92%
     2         22     92.71%    95.83%    92.64%   92.01%
     3         21     89.21%    93.75%    89.06%   88.26%
     5         19     82.52%    89.58%    82.12%   81.25%
     8         16     73.19%    83.33%    72.22%   71.85%
    10         14     67.42%    79.17%    65.97%   66.29%
    15          9     54.49%    68.75%    51.56%   54.59%
    21          3     41.54%    56.25%    36.56%   44.08%

  How to read this: If you block 5 curses, ~17.5% of relics that would
  otherwise match your passive criteria will be rejected due to carrying
  a blocked curse. The Odds Viewer automatically factors this in.

  Why Grand is sometimes higher than Polished with many blocked curses:
  Grand relics are 40% curse-free (vs 30% for Polished), so a larger
  fraction of them always pass. But Grand has more curse slots when cursed,
  so the pass rate drops faster at high exclusion counts. The crossover
  depends on how many curses you block.

SECTION G — THE CATEGORY EXCLUSION EFFECT (ODDS SHIFTING) [PM]
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This section explains the most counter-intuitive aspect of relic probability.

─── G1. WHAT IS CATEGORY EXCLUSION? ─────────────────────────────────

Each passive belongs to a category (e.g., "Attack Power", "HP Management").
When a passive is drawn in one slot, ALL OTHER PASSIVES in that same category
are eliminated from the remaining slots.

Example: If slot 1 draws "Fire Attack Power Up +3", then slot 2 cannot
draw any other "Attack Power" passive. The entire category is removed.

This elimination happens BEFORE slot 2 draws — the effective pool for
slot 2 is smaller, which changes the probability of everything else.

─── G2. HOW ELIMINATION RAISES ODDS (THE BOOST) ────────────────────

When a category is eliminated from the pool:
  - The total pool weight decreases
  - All SURVIVING passives get a proportional probability boost
  - Your target passive (if it survived) benefits from this boost

Boost formula:
  New probability = w(T) / (W - W(eliminated category))

Example:
  Pool W = 10,000, eliminated category weight = 500, target weight = 100
  Before:  100 / 10,000 = 1.00%
  After:   100 /  9,500 = 1.05%   → +0.05% boost

─── G3. THE DOUBLE-EDGE EFFECT [PM] ─────────────────────────────────

Here is the critical insight that makes this LESS GOOD than it sounds:

When your target passive T gets a probability boost from category elimination,
ALL OF T'S CATEGORY SIBLINGS GET THE EXACT SAME PROPORTIONAL BOOST.

"Siblings" = other passives in the same category as T.
If a sibling is drawn in a later slot, it ELIMINATES T from that slot forward.

  For every +X% boost T gains, siblings collectively gain +(N-1)×X%
  where N is the number of passives in T's category.

  If T's category has 10 members (T + 9 siblings):
    T gains +0.10% → siblings collectively gain +0.90%
    The risk that a sibling blocks T in the NEXT slot grew 9x faster.

─── G4. WHEN DOES ELIMINATION PURELY HELP? ─────────────────────────

  FINAL SLOT: Category elimination is ALWAYS helpful.
    No future draws remain. T can only benefit from the smaller pool.

  INTERMEDIATE SLOTS: It is a MIXED outcome.
    Pro: T's probability in this slot is higher.
    Con: Sibling probability in LATER slots is proportionally higher too.
    Net result is complex and depends on the full probability tree.

  KEY INSIGHT: On a 3-slot Grand relic, getting a "useful" category eliminated
  in slot 1 helps slot 2, but the increased sibling risk in slot 2 partially
  offsets the gain. On the FINAL slot (slot 2 for Polished, slot 3 for Grand),
  elimination is always net positive.

─── G5. THE "FREE SLOT" PASSIVE PROBLEM ─────────────────────────────

Some passives belong to the "C_special" category (exact category TBD).
These passives use a slot WITHOUT eliminating their category.
This is strictly bad for your target on intermediate slots:
  - You lost a slot (cannot get T in this slot)
  - The pool barely shrank (only the exact drawn passive removed, not the whole category)
  - All categories including T's siblings remain fully available next slot

A C_special draw in slot 1 is the near-worst-case outcome for T's odds
on the remaining slots of a multi-passive relic.

─── G6. PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS ─────────────────────────────────────

1. MORE SLOTS = BETTER. Grand relics give T 3 chances instead of 1.
   Even with the category interactions, more slots almost always help.

2. CATEGORY WEIGHT MATTERS. If T's category is heavy (many siblings, high
   total weight), the double-edge is more dangerous. A category of 2
   passives is much less risky than one with 20 passives.

3. ON THE FINAL SLOT, ELIMINATION ONLY HELPS. If you see a relic with
   2 slots filled and neither is your target, the effective pool for slot 3
   is smaller and T's relative weight is higher. The bad news came in slots 1-2
   (lost draws), not in the final slot itself.

4. COMPAT CONFLICTS = ZERO CHANCE. If you want two passives in the same
   compat category, P = 0. It is impossible by game design.

5. FOR DEEP RELICS: The exclusive pool (TABLE_2000000) and broad pool
   (TABLE_2100000) are SEPARATE draws — T from one pool does not compete
   with T from the other pool. The pools themselves are independent.

SECTION I — QUICK REFERENCE FORMULAS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

P(T on relic) = (probability that a random purchase has passive T)

For Deep relics (9-variant model):
  P(T) = SUM over all 9 variants: P(variant) × P(T in any slot of variant)

  Where P(T in any slot) uses the inclusion-exclusion formula:
    For K slots with per-slot probabilities p1, p2, ..., pK:
    P(T in at least one slot) = 1 - (1-p1)(1-p2)...(1-pK)

P(T1 AND T2 on same relic) — must use inclusion-exclusion, NOT multiplication.
  Events are correlated through category exclusion.
  The engine handles this automatically.

P(at least 1 success in N relics) = 1 - (1 - P(T))^N

Expected relics to first match = 1 / P(T)

P(relic passes curse filter | k curses blocked) =
  SUM over all variants: P(variant) × ((24 - k) / 24)^(curse_slots in variant)

  where ^0 = 1 (variants with 0 curse slots always pass)

Effective probability with curse filter:
  P_effective = P(passive match) × P(curse filter passes)
  (These are independent given relic variant selection)
